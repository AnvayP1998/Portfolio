    Being visible online is more important than ever ‚Äî especially for students building careers in business, tech, or consulting. But you don't need to be a graphic designer or social media guru to make a strong impression.

    Here are 5 tools and tips that have worked for me and other GW students:

    1. Create a Personal Landing Page

    Sites like GitHub Pages or Notion are great for setting up a simple, one-page portfolio. Link your resume, projects, and even fun facts about your GW journey.

    2. Design Clean Social Graphics with Canva

    From club invites to case comp promos, Canva's free student templates help you stay consistent and branded without starting from scratch.

    3. Use LinkedIn for More Than Job Hunting

    Post updates about projects, events you attend, or student org wins. Tag GW and your peers to grow your network.

    4. Build a Content Calendar (Even if It's Just for You)

    Scheduling your posts helps keep your online voice active and professional. I use Google Sheets with simple columns: Date, Topic, Channel, Link.

    5. Write Like You Speak (But Spellcheck!)

    Authenticity beats perfection. Whether it's a student newsletter blurb or an IG caption, write like you'd talk to a smart friend ‚Äî and then proofread twice.
